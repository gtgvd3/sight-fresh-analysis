## The Ingredients: What's Actually Inside

This is where Sight Fresh earns its keep — or doesn't. Let's go through the main players.

**Bilberry Extract** is the headline ingredient. Rich in anthocyanins, bilberry has been studied for its role in rhodopsin regeneration — the pigment your retinas use to process low-light conditions. Over 15 published studies have examined bilberry's effects on visual function, with research documenting improvements in night vision, reduced eye fatigue, and enhanced retinal blood flow, according to a review aggregated on ResearchGate.

**Lutein (20mg)** acts as a natural blue-light filter in the macula. According to a JAMA study cited on the NIH's PubMed database, lutein and zeaxanthin are directly associated with reduced risk of age-related macular degeneration (AMD). More than 30 peer-reviewed studies have investigated lutein's role in eye health, consistently showing increased macular pigment density and protection against blue light damage.

**Zeaxanthin** works alongside lutein — the two carotenoids essentially team up to protect the central retina. You rarely see one without the other in well-formulated eye supplements.

**Eyebright (Euphrasia)** has a long history of traditional use for eye irritation and inflammation. It's in here to soothe the surface-level discomfort that comes from dry, strained eyes.

**Vitamins C and E** provide antioxidant coverage. The NIH's landmark AREDS2 study (Age-Related Eye Disease Study) found that supplementing with vitamin C, vitamin E, lutein, and zeaxanthin significantly reduced the risk of AMD progression in at-risk adults.

**Zinc and Copper** round out the formula. Zinc helps transport vitamin A from the liver to the retina to produce protective melanin, and copper is included because high zinc doses can deplete copper — so they're paired intentionally.

That's a well-considered stack. Nothing exotic or unproven. These are all ingredients with documented mechanisms of action, not proprietary mystery blends.
